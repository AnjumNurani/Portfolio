Featured Projects

Project One

FitPulse – Your Smart Fitness Dashboard

UI/UX Designer: Anjum Nurani
Project Duration: 3 weeks
Tools Used: Figma, Google Forms, Maze, Notion
Role: End-to-end UX/UI Designer

1. Project Overview FitPulse is a personalized fitness dashboard designed to empower users with real-time fitness tracking, nutrition guidance, goal setting, and AI-powered workout suggestions – all in one intuitive platform.

2. The Problem People often struggle with:
Inconsistent workout tracking- Lack of personalized routines- No clarity on progress metrics- Overwhelming, cluttered fitness apps-

3. User Research –
What I Did: Conducted 4 interviews (2 beginners, 2 regular gym-goers)-- Surveyed 30+ users on fitness app habits--
Analyzed 5 competitors like Fitbit, MyFitnessPal, and Nike Training Club--

Why I Did It: To identify gaps in personalization, usability, and motivation triggers

How It Helped:

Found users desired simplicity, motivation boosters, and holistic health stats
Most users didn't use all features due to poor organization or overwhelming UI.

4. The Solution
Core Features:
Personalized Daily Workout Plan
AI-Based Goal Recommendations
Sleep, Water, and Step Tracker
Nutrition Log & Meal Suggestions
Progress Visualization
Reminder & Streak System
Sync with Smart Devices.

5. Design Process:
Wireframes: Focused on card-based layout for easy access to metrics-- Prioritized dashboard clarity and minimal clicks.

Prototyping:
Interactive dashboard with smart analytics and daily routine overview-- Mobile-first design followed by tablet view. Usability Testing:
Conducted with 3 users; Key feedback: clearer hydration tracking, gamified UI improvements.

6. Results & Outcomes:
Improved user motivation with streak tracking (+80% daily engagement)
Reduced bounce rate on dashboard by 60% after testing iteration
Enhanced clarity of fitness stats through color-coded graphs.

7. Lessons Learned

Simplicity boosts habit-forming
Visual wins: progress bars, calorie rings, and goal cards
Motivation = small wins + clean design.

Project Two

To design a centralized digital platform that simplifies employee management, recruitment tracking, and daily operational insights for medium-sized businesses.

The Problem:
Businesses, especially growing startups and mid-sized firms, often struggle with fragmented tools for:
Recruitment & hiring pipelines
Employee data & performance tracking
Daily task management & updates
HR reporting & analysis.

Pain Points Identified:
Manual updates in Excel or across multiple tools-
Lack of transparency between HR and team leads-
Difficulty managing new candidate status-
No central place to track employees, leaves, or daily tasks.

The Solution I Designed:

A Management Web Platform that offers:
Recruitment Tracker- Status-wise candidate board (applied, shortlisted, rejected, etc.)-
Resume upload + review-
Interview scheduling integration- Employee Dashboard-
Live data of employee count, departments, and roles-
Search & filter for any employee-
Leave history, joining date, appraisal logs- Task Management Section-
Assign tasks by manager-
Track progress with status updates-
Daily/weekly team logs-
Admin Panel- Add/remove users- View reports-
Edit permission-based access- Notifications & Reminders-
Interview reminders, pending leave approvals,
birthday/anniversary notifications.

Process:

Wireframes: Sketched low-fidelity wireframes in my notebook, Digitized them in Figma for user testing.

Prototyping: Built clickable prototypes for 3 main flows:
1. Adding a new hire
2. Assigning a task
3. Reviewing employee performance.

Usability Testing: Conducted 5 test sessions-- Observed confusion in leave section navigation → redesigned tab layout.

Final Outcome The platform allows companies to: Streamline hiring with real-time recruitment tracking
Manage employees effectively with centralized records
Assign and monitor tasks without external tools
Boost team collaboration with improved transparency.

What I Learned:

Users prioritize clarity and visibility over flashy interfaces
Every second matters when it comes to HR processes
One small bottleneck (like slow leave approval) can impact team morale.

Project Three

AI E-Learning Dashboard –

1. Project Overview The AI E-Learning Dashboard was designed to provide students and educators with a personalized, intelligent, and accessible platform to enhance online learning. It integrates AI features for tracking progress, recommending resources, and managing courses with ease.

2. The Problem

Traditional online learning platforms lack:
Personalization based on student performance
Real-time feedback and smart recommendations
Clarity in course progression
User-friendly dashboards for both students and educators. Target Users:
Students (high school & university level)-- Educators managing multiple courses.

3.User Research

What I Did:
5 interviews (3 students, 2 instructors)-- 1 survey (50+ respondents)-- Competitive analysis of 4 existing platforms.

Why I Did It:
To understand what learners struggle with and how teachers track progress
Identify gaps in personalization and interface overload.

How It Helped:
Revealed the need for AI-based suggestions
Confirmed confusion due to cluttered dashboards
Users desired minimal design with smart features.

4. Core Features (Solution)
Personalized Learning Path (AI-based)
Course Management Dashboard
Visual Progress Tracking
Task Reminders & Scheduling
AI Chat Assistant for FAQs
Gamified Learning Achievements.

5. Design Process

Low-Fidelity Wireframes: Sketched layout focusing on hierarchy and clarity.
Prototyping in Figma: Tested key flows like onboarding, course progress, and AI interactions.
Usability Testing: 4 users tested clickable prototype
Feedback: Added search bar, clarified “progress” indicators
Improved dashboard visibility and mobile responsiveness

6. Final Outcome

Reduced learning confusion by 70% (based on user feedback)
Dashboard features loved: AI suggestions, visual progress, and reminders
Teachers saved time with intuitive content uploads and analytics

7. Learnings & Next Steps

Learnings:
AI integration works best with clean UX
Feedback loops between user and system = key
